# Competitor & Category Scanner

Map the landscape to find strategic space. Understand category dynamics, identify trends, and spot positioning opportunities.

## Analysis Framework

### Category Overview

**Market definition**:
- What category/market are we analysing?
- Geographic scope
- Timeframe for analysis

**Market dynamics**:
- Market size and growth trajectory
- Key drivers of category growth/decline
- Regulatory or structural factors
- Recent disruptions or shifts

### Competitive Landscape

**Player mapping**:

| Competitor | Market Position | Core Offering | Target Audience | Key Differentiator |
|------------|-----------------|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| [Name] | [Leader/Challenger/Niche] | [What they sell] | [Who they target] | [Their main claim] |

**Market structure**:
- Leaders (dominant share, set category norms)
- Challengers (growing share, disrupting)
- Niche players (specialised positioning)
- New entrants (emerging threats/opportunities)

### Positioning Analysis

**Positioning map**: Plot competitors on two key dimensions relevant to category

Common dimensions:
- Price <-> Premium
- Traditional <-> Innovative
- Mass market <-> Niche
- Functional <-> Emotional
- Local <-> Global

**White space identification**:
- Where are there gaps in the positioning map?
- Which audience needs are underserved?
- What positioning territories are overcrowded?

### Messaging Analysis

For each major competitor, capture:

**Primary claim**: What's their main message?
**Proof points**: How do they support it?
**Tone**: How do they sound?
**Channels**: Where do they show up?

**Messaging patterns**:
- What themes dominate the category?
- What language is everyone using?
- What claims are table stakes vs. differentiating?

### Trend Identification

**Category trends**:
- Emerging themes in competitor activity
- Shifts in customer expectations
- Channel/platform evolution
- Technology or product innovation

**Cultural trends**:
- Broader societal shifts affecting category
- Changing values or priorities
- Economic or demographic shifts

**Communications trends**:
- Evolving media landscape
- New channels or formats
- Influencer/creator economy shifts

### SWOT by Competitor

For key competitors:

| | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--|-----------|------------|
| **Internal** | [Capabilities, resources, brand equity] | [Gaps, limitations, vulnerabilities] |

| | Opportunities | Threats |
|--|--------------|---------|
| **External** | [Market shifts they could exploit] | [Risks they face] |

## Research Methods

### Desk Research Sources

**Company information**:
- Corporate websites and newsrooms
- Annual reports (listed companies)
- LinkedIn company pages
- Crunchbase, PitchBook (if relevant)

**Media presence**:
- Google News search (last 12 months)
- Trade publication coverage
- Podcast appearances

**Digital footprint**:
- Social media channels (content, engagement, growth)
- SEO tools (traffic, keywords)
- Review sites (sentiment, themes)

**Industry context**:
- Industry reports (IBISWorld, Statista)
- Trade body publications
- Conference presentations

### Social Listening

Monitor for:
- Brand mentions and sentiment
- Category conversation themes
- Emerging issues or opportunities
- Influencer activity

### Content Audit

Review competitor content across:
- Website messaging
- Social media themes
- PR and media coverage
- Advertising (if visible)
- Thought leadership

## Output Formats

### Landscape Overview (Presentation)

1. Executive summary
2. Market context
3. Competitive map
4. Positioning analysis
5. Messaging themes
6. Trend spotlight
7. Opportunity areas
8. Recommendations

### Competitor Matrix (Document)

Detailed comparison table covering:
- Company overview
- Positioning
- Target audience
- Key messages
- Channels
- Recent activity
- Strengths/weaknesses

### Trend Brief (One-pager)

- Trend definition
- Evidence/examples
- Implications for client
- Recommended response

## Strategic Implications

Always conclude with:

**Where to play**: Which positioning territory offers best opportunity?

**How to win**: What would it take to own that space?

**Watch points**: What trends or competitors to monitor?

**Recommended actions**: Specific next steps

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Category boundaries clearly defined
- [ ] All major competitors included
- [ ] Data sources documented
- [ ] Positioning map based on relevant dimensions
- [ ] Trends supported by evidence
- [ ] White space identified
- [ ] Implications are actionable
- [ ] Recency of data noted
