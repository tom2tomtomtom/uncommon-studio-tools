# Competitor Analysis (Deep-Dive)

Go deep on a single competitor to understand their strategy, strengths, and vulnerabilities. This is intelligence for competitive advantage.

## Analysis Framework

### Company Overview

**Basics**:
- Company name and ownership structure
- Founding date and key milestones
- Leadership team
- Size (employees, revenue if available)
- Geographic presence

**Business model**:
- Core products/services
- Revenue streams
- Target market segments
- Distribution/channel strategy

**Recent developments**:
- Last 12-18 months of news
- Strategic shifts
- Leadership changes
- Product launches
- Partnerships or acquisitions

### Brand Positioning

**Brand promise**: What do they claim to deliver?

**Positioning statement** (reverse-engineered):
"For [target audience], [Brand] is the [category] that [key benefit] because [reason to believe]."

**Brand personality**:
- How do they want to be perceived?
- What tone do they use?
- What visual identity signals?

**Differentiation**: What makes them distinct from others in category?

**Proof points**: What evidence do they offer?

### Messaging Analysis

**Primary message**: The core claim across all communications

**Supporting messages**: Secondary claims and proof points

**Message architecture**:

| Audience | Primary Message | Supporting Messages |
|----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| [Segment 1] | [Headline message] | [Supporting points] |
| [Segment 2] | [Headline message] | [Supporting points] |

**Messaging evolution**: How has their messaging changed over time?

**Message effectiveness**: What seems to resonate? What falls flat?

### Communications Strategy

**Channel presence**:

| Channel | Presence | Activity Level | Content Type | Engagement |
|---------|----------|----------------|--------------|------------|
| Website | [URL] | [High/Med/Low] | [Types] | [Metrics if visible] |
| LinkedIn | [Handle] | [Posts/week] | [Types] | [Avg engagement] |
| Instagram | [Handle] | [Posts/week] | [Types] | [Avg engagement] |
| Twitter/X | [Handle] | [Posts/day] | [Types] | [Avg engagement] |
| TikTok | [Handle] | [Posts/week] | [Types] | [Avg engagement] |

**Content themes**: What do they talk about most?

**Tone analysis**: How do they sound? (Formal/casual, expert/accessible, etc.)

**Campaign activity**: Notable campaigns in last 12 months

### Media Presence

**Earned media analysis**:
- Volume of coverage (last 12 months)
- Key outlets securing coverage
- Spokesperson visibility
- Story angles gaining traction
- Sentiment overview

**Paid media** (if visible):
- Advertising platforms
- Campaign themes
- Spending indicators (if available)

**Owned media**:
- Blog/content hub activity
- Thought leadership output
- Case studies and resources

### Share of Voice

**Coverage volume comparison** (vs. client and other competitors):

| Brand | Coverage Count | Est. Reach | Sentiment | SOV % |
|-------|---------------|------------|-----------|-------|
| Competitor | [#] | [#] | [%] | [%] |
| Client | [#] | [#] | [%] | [%] |

**Share of conversation** (social/digital):
- Volume of brand mentions
- Engagement comparison
- Topic ownership

### Strengths & Vulnerabilities

**Strengths**:
- What they do well
- Advantages they hold
- Positive perceptions

Example format:
| Strength | Evidence | Threat Level to Client |
|----------|----------|------------------------|
| [Strength] | [How we know] | [High/Med/Low] |

**Vulnerabilities**:
- Weaknesses in offering or positioning
- Gaps in communications
- Reputational risks
- Negative sentiment themes

Example format:
| Vulnerability | Evidence | Opportunity for Client |
|---------------|----------|------------------------|
| [Weakness] | [How we know] | [High/Med/Low] |

### Strategic Implications

**Competitive threats**:
- Where are they winning against us?
- What should we watch?
- What moves might they make?

**Opportunities for client**:
- Positioning territories they've left open
- Weaknesses we can exploit
- Messages we can counter
- Audiences they're neglecting

**Recommended actions**:
- Specific tactical recommendations
- Defensive moves to consider
- Offensive opportunities

## Research Sources

### Primary Sources
- Corporate website (About, Newsroom, Careers)
- Annual reports (if public company)
- LinkedIn company page and executive profiles
- Social media channels (content audit)
- Press releases
- Podcasts/interviews with leadership

### Media & Coverage
- Google News (last 12-18 months)
- Trade publications
- Industry awards submissions/wins
- Event speaking slots

### Third-Party Intelligence
- Review sites (Glassdoor, G2, Trustpilot)
- SEO tools (traffic, keywords, backlinks)
- Social listening platforms
- Industry analyst reports

### Inference and Analysis
- Job postings (indicate strategic priorities)
- Patent filings (if relevant)
- Partnership announcements
- Customer case studies

## Output Formats

### Full Deep-Dive Report
Comprehensive document covering all sections (15-25 pages)

### Executive Brief
One-page summary with key findings and strategic implications

### Competitor Card
Quick reference sheet with essential information for ongoing use

### Battle Card
Tactical guide for sales/BD team on how to compete against this rival

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] All claims sourced and dated
- [ ] Multiple data sources used
- [ ] Messaging reverse-engineered from evidence
- [ ] Strengths and weaknesses balanced and evidenced
- [ ] Strategic implications specific to client
- [ ] Recommendations actionable
- [ ] Recency of information noted
