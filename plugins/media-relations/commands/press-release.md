# /press-release

Write a professional press release.

## What this does
Creates a complete, AP-style press release ready to distribute, with headline, subhead, body, quotes, and boilerplate.

## When to use
- Announcing news (product launch, partnership, executive hire)
- Making something official
- Need a polished, professional format

## How to use
Share:
- What's the news?
- Why does it matter?
- Any quotes you want included (or who should be quoted)
- Key facts and details

## Example
```
/press-release

Announcing: We just hired Jane Smith as our new CMO. She was previously VP Marketing at BigCorp for 8 years. CEO should be quoted. She starts March 1st. We're a 500-person SaaS company in Chicago.
```

## What you'll get
- **Headline** - News-focused, attention-grabbing
- **Subhead** - Additional context
- **Lead paragraph** - Who, what, when, where, why
- **Supporting details** - The full story
- **Quotes** - From key stakeholders
- **Boilerplate** - About your organization
- **Contact info** - Media contact details (you fill in)
